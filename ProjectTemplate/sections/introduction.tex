\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:Introduction}
\blindtext

The main contributions of this work are the following.
On the one hand, it serves as a suitable \LaTeX\ template (with reasonable formatting options) that can be used for academic papers.
On the other hand, it provides a loose guideline on how one might arrange and structure such a work along with some concrete examples, e.g., mathematical formulations and figures.

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows.
We begin in Section \ref{section:kp} with a short introduction to the Knapsack Problem.
In more detail, we discuss a mathematical formulation, the computational complexity, and dynamic programming approaches.
Afterwards, in Section \ref{section:tsp}, we dedicate ourselves to the Traveling Salesman Problem.
Finally, concluding remarks will be drawn in Section \ref{section:conclusion}.
